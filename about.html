All About Kauri Dieback

What is Kauri Dieback?

Kauri Dieback is a devastating disease caused by a microscopic fungus-like organism called Phytophthora agathidicida. It affects the roots of kauri trees, leading to rotting of the root system and eventual death of the tree. The disease spreads through soil movement and can be transmitted by human activities.

Impact on Kauri Trees

Kauri trees are iconic to New Zealand's forests, playing a significant role in the ecosystem and cultural heritage of the region. The loss of kauri trees due to Kauri Dieback has profound ecological and cultural impacts, affecting biodiversity, water quality, and cultural practices.

Prevention and Management

Preventing the spread of Kauri Dieback is essential to protect remaining kauri trees. Measures such as cleaning footwear, staying on designated tracks, and avoiding areas with infected trees can help minimize the spread of the disease. Management efforts include track upgrades, hygiene stations, and research to understand the disease better.

Get Involved

You can help protect kauri trees by raising awareness about Kauri Dieback, following biosecurity guidelines when visiting kauri forests, and supporting conservation efforts. By working together, we can preserve these majestic trees for future generations.

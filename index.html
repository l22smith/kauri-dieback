Save the Kauri Trees


Kauri dieback was first noticed on Great Barrier Island in the 1970s. It was thought to be a fungus with little risk to kauri.



Kauri Dieback is a devastating disease caused by a microscopic fungus-like organism called Phytophthora agathidicida.
It infects the roots and damages the tissues that carry nutrients within the tree.
This leads to the death of the tree. Kauri trees are iconic to New Zealand's forests, and their loss would have profound ecological and cultural impacts.
